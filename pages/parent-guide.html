Everything parents and guardians need to know about Happy Games
👋 Welcome, Parents & Guardians

Happy Games is a completely free, ad-free educational website designed for children aged 4–11. It is built to support the UK primary school curriculum and help children learn through play. This guide explains how it works and how you can keep your child's experience safe.

🎮 What is Happy Games?
📚12 quiz subjects aligned to the UK primary curriculum (English, Maths, Science, Grammar, Spelling, and more)
🎲Board and arcade games: Chess, Tic-Tac-Toe, Memory, Ludo, Snakes & Ladders, and more
🌐Online multiplayer games your child can play with friends using a private room code
🧠A Feelings & Coping Quest to support emotional wellbeing and resilience
📊Progress tracking — see your child's quiz scores and history
🏆Leaderboard to encourage friendly competition
🛡️ Is it Safe for My Child?

Yes. Happy Games is built with child safety as the priority:

  • ✅ No adverts — the site is completely ad-free
  • ✅ No in-app purchases — it is free, always
  • ✅ No open chat — players cannot type messages to each other
  • ✅ No real names required — only a username and display name
  • ✅ No profile photos — no images of children are uploaded or displayed
  • ✅ No location data — we never ask for or store locations
  • ✅ Private multiplayer rooms — joined only with a shared 6-letter code
  • ✅ Minimum data collection — only what is needed for the service
📝 Account Creation

Children under 13 should create an account with their parent or guardian's knowledge. The sign-up process requires:

  • A username (letters, numbers, underscores only — not a real name). We recommend a nickname.
  • A display name (the name shown in games — can also be a nickname)
  • A password (minimum 6 characters)

Our recommendation: Help your child choose their username and password. Write it down and keep it somewhere safe.

🗄️ What Data Is Stored?

We store:

  • Username and password (password is stored as a secure hash — we cannot read it)
  • Display name (the name your child chose)
  • Quiz scores and game history
  • Year/level selection (Year 1–6)

We do NOT store: real names, email addresses, phone numbers, home addresses, profile photos, or location data.

🌐 Multiplayer Safety

Some games support online multiplayer. Here is how it works:

  • One player creates a private room and gets a 6-letter code
  • The other player joins using that code — like a digital playdate
  • There is no open text chat — players interact only through gameplay
  • Players are identified by display name only (no real name, no photos)
  • Room codes expire automatically after inactivity

We recommend children only share room codes with friends they know in real life.

🗑️ How to Delete Your Child's Account

You can request full account deletion at any time. There are two ways:

  1. Self-service: If your child knows their password, visit our Delete Account page and follow the instructions.
  2. Email request: Email info@ziontechnologyltd.com with the subject "Delete Account — [Username]". We will delete the account and all associated data within 30 days.

Deletion removes: the account login, the username reservation, and all quiz scores and game history.

⚖️ Your Rights

As a parent or guardian, you have the right to:

  • Access the data we hold about your child
  • Request corrections to inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your child's account and all data
  • Object to how we process your child's data

To exercise any of these rights, email info@ziontechnologyltd.com with the subject "Data Request — [Username]".

⏱️ Screen Time Recommendation

Happy Games is most effective in short, focused sessions of 15–30 minutes. We recommend parental guidance and co-play for younger children (ages 4–6). For older children (ages 7–11), the platform can be used more independently as a homework supplement or revision tool.
